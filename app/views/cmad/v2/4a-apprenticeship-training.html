How your apprenticeship will be delivered

Your apprenticeship will be delivered in two parts:

  • a training period where you may move between different placements
  • an end-point assessment

Your training period

Your employer will arrange placements where you will gain hands-on experience in your chosen occupation.

Each placement will help you develop some of the skills required for your apprenticeship.

Your training provider is responsible for your off-the-job training. Your required hours for off-the-job training are stated in your training plan. Youâ€™ll spend this time learning new knowledge, skills and behaviours for your apprenticeship. Your training provider will manage your off-the-job training.

Your training plan is agreed between you and your training provider. The plan will set out a training schedule for your off-the-job training. Your training plan will include:

  • the delivery of the knowledge, skills and behaviours required for your apprenticeship
  • how and when your off-the-job training will be delivered
  • English and maths qualifications, if you have not already achieved these
  • regular progress reviews with your employer and training provider

Your end-point assessment

You'll be assessed by an independent end-point assessor on the knowledge, skills and behaviours that you've learnt during your apprenticeship to confirm you're occupationally competent.

You will agree when you're ready for your end-point assessment in your progress reviews with your employer and training provider.

Your employer and training provider will tell you what to expect and when the assessment will take place.
